Overview

Andrew is an avid problem solver dedicated to addressing important environmental issues related to water, waste, and agriculture. During his formative years, Andrew held positions at the US Environmental Protection Agency researching water quality in pipe distribution networks, and at BiOWiSH Technologies. BiOWiSH allowed Andrew to model the impacts of differing soil microbiomes on agricultural yields and carved out the path for him to become a data scientist. At BiOWiSH, Andrew informed senior level management about his predictions regarding the potential size and importance markets across the globe using agricultural production datasets. Andrewâ€™s desire to become a data scientist was inspired at BiOWiSH because it connected the three fields that he is most passionate about: engineering, environmental science, and microbiology.

Andrew is also the vice president of Resilient Communities, a nonprofit organization developing waste to energy technologies in impoverished communities around the world. The organization is currently pursuing a research project in Marrakech, Morocco aimed at developing technology to recover methane from wastewater and provide it to a nearby community. He also headed a team that design a methane capture device for a wastewater treatment lagoon.

When Andrew is not trying to save the world, he enjoys baking sourdough bread using his home-grown yeast culture. He also enjoys cooking and tinkering with Indian food recipes inspired by his various excursions to the country. Andrew can be found pedaling his bicycle around town hunting for the best coffee beans to take home to his French press. He may also be spotted tending to his herb garden trying to harvest the best herbs to bake his next loaf of bread.
